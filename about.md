<section class="about-section">

Hey, I'm Jin. I'm a tinkerer at heart who spent a large chunk of my career in enterprise DevOps and cloud operations, mostly in the healthcare IT sector. These days I'm deep in the agentic AI wave along with everyone else, exploring what agents, copilots, and new developer tooling actually look like in practice. I love a flashy demo as much as anyone, but I also care about whether something holds up against real enterprise requirements.

I genuinely believe we're living through *the* transformative moment. Everything is accelerating at a pace that brings incredibly powerful advancements and fascinating possibilities, but also real uncertainty and hard questions about our role as a species going forward. It's equal parts thrilling and humbling.

But really, I'm just another guy who spends too much time in front of a monitor, who decided to create a blog. I practice Muay Thai, I'm married, I have two cats, and I'm based out of Chicago.

</section>

<section class="about-section">

<h2>Why this site exists</h2>

This site is a place to think out loud a bit more than I normally would.

I'm not a prolific writer and I don't write just to produce content. But some ideas are worth slowing down for, especially when things are moving this fast and the implications are this big.

Operator Notes is where I put those ideas. It's more workbench than publication. Expect rough edges, honest takes, and practical notes from whatever I'm tinkering with.

</section>
